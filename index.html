Voice

The essence of who we are comes from our purpose to deliver happiness to our clients. But that’s not just lip service: it’s something we live by here at Legwork. Employees lend a hand when needed. If someone is feeling overwhelmed, others step in and help. Our voice as a company should exemplify the intrinsic nature of how we treat others. This means writing for Legwork should be clear, friendly, and straightforward. We want to be helpful, but not overbearing, expert, yet modest. Above all, we aim to treat people with respect and cultivate happiness.

The Legwork Logo

The Legwork logo is the focal point of our brand. Therefore, it’s important to retain it’s integrity and use it exactly as specified in the guidelines below.

The Legwork Smile

Sometimes, it may be preferred to use the Legwork “Smile” on it’s own, rather than the full logo: predominately, when the Legwork brand has already been established through the use of the full logo, copy, or vocally.

Futhermore, there are instances where we may want to use the Legwork Smile independently, such as apparel, stickers, or other merchandise– instances where we want the graphics to be the main point of focus, and the icon to act as a visual reminder.

Exclusion Zone

To ensure legibility, our logo must stand out among other elements. This is achieved by surrounding our logo with clear space. The logo and the smile icon’s exclusion zone is equal to half the height of the icon (marked as × in the diagram).

Minimum Size

Never reduce the Logo or Smile to a size smaller that the following recommendations, as it can result in loss of readibility and impact.

The Legwork logo should never be smaller than 70px in digital or 20mm in print.

The Legwork Smile icon should never be smaller than 21px in digital or 6mm in print.

Color Palette

Our colors help define value and strengthen our purpose to deliver happiness both inside and outside of the company. Designs created for our brand should be inviting, yet professional. Tints of colors are permitted as long as the integrity of the color is maintained. Deep Sea should be treated as our brand’s black.

Gradients

Gradients can be added to provide a sense of energy. Be cautious not to overuse gradients and be sure to always use flat colors as a fallback on the web.

What to Avoid

There are endless possbilities when it comes to color, which also means there are endless “don’ts.” A general rule of thumb is to never use color on color. If you’re using a colored background, don’t use colored text or icons.

  • Color text on Color Background

    Don’t use color on top of color. If you have a colored background, use white text.

  • Color Icons on Color Background

    Same with icons, use white icons if you have a colored background.

General Typography Rules

Tracking (letter-spacing) and leading (line-height) both play crucial roles in creating the spacious and professional look throughout Legwork’s content. Be sure to give text plenty of breathing room; but, be cognizant of how you use it. A few things to watch out for:

  • Don't write headings, CTA's, links, or body copy in all caps or smallcaps
  • Never justify text
  • Don't alter the widths of letters
  • Only use brand-approved fonts

Overlays in Use

Overlays, when executed correctly, can add value and human connection. Please adhere to the guidelines below to ensure proper usage:

  • Do use white text and CTA's on overlays
  • Do make sure there's plenty of room for text
  • Do maintain the integrity of the image
  • Do bring down the saturation to near black and white behind the overlay
  • Don't use color on color
  • Don't crop or distort the photo in a way that cuts off faces
  • Don't choose a photo that's too busy with faces
  • Don't bring up the saturation behind the overlay
